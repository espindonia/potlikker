Blackberry Cobbler

A classic Southern dessert, best paired with ice cream.

What you need:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cups AP flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • A pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cups of milk
  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 3-4 cups of blackberries
  • 3/4 cups of sugar

Steps:

  1. Mix sugar, flour, baking powder & salt. Add milk and mix well.
  2. Melt butter in a 9x13 dish.
  3. Pour flour batter over butter (DO NOT STIR). Mix blackberries & sugar and water to cover. Mix till sugar dissolves - pour over batter (DO NOT STIR).
  4. Bake - 350 for 1 hour

Nestle Toll House Cookies

Copyright who? Pairs well with a glass of milk.

What you need:

  • 2 1/4 cups AP flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) softened butter
  • 3/4 cup granduated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups (12-oz bag) chocolate chips
  • 1 cup nuts, chopped (optional)

Steps:

  1. Combine flour, soda & salt in bowl.
  2. Beat butter, sugar, and vanilla in large bowl.
  3. Beat in eggs, then flour mixture.
  4. Stir in chocolate chips and nuts.
  5. Drop by tablespoons onto a cookie sheet.
  6. Bake 375 for 9-11 minutes.

Peanut Butter Cookies

Makes good cookie sandwiches, but also is good by itself.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups AP flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 1/4 cups dark brown sugar, firmly packed
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups white sugar
  • 1 cup salted butter
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 300.
  2. Combine flour, soda, salt in bowl. Mix well, set aside.
  3. Blend sugars in mixer on medium speed. Add butter till grainy paste.
  4. Add eggs, peanut butter, vanilla, and mix till light and fluffy.
  5. Add flour mix till mixed.
  6. Drop by tbsp onto sheet, bake 18-22 minutes.
  7. Press with fork to form criss-cross pattern, then let cool.

Strawberry Jelly

Simple, if you freeze it it'll last a couple years.

Ingredients:

(Measure with your heart)

  • Strawberries, fresh is best
  • Sugar, white works best
  • Optional: a small amount of gelatin, dissolved/dilluted in water

Steps:

  1. Clean and chop strawberries, mix in sugar and let sit for ~10 minutes. It is okay to eat now as a topping for desserts and bland cereal.
  2. To make a jelly, take a pot and set the berries simmer on medium heat until soft and lightly bubbly.
  3. Mash the berries when soft, optional: add gelatin now.
  4. Store in mason jars or tupperware, if you aren't using it soon put it in the freezer.

Cucumber Salad

Not really a salad, but it helps digestion.

Ingredients:

(Measure to taste/by heart)

  • Cucumbers, cleaned and sliced
  • Vinegar, preferably distilled white
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Italian herb mix OR ranch salad dressing mix
  • Parsley
  • Honey or white sugar (optional)
  • Oil, olive is best
  • Additional vegetables (carrots, celery, etc) if desired

Steps:

  1. Mix all ingredients into a large bowl, there should be enough vinegar to cover all of the vegetables (like if you were pickling).
  2. If you can't handle too much acid, use the sugar or honey and add extra oil to help the vinegar go down easier
  3. Either serve now, or cover it and set it aside in the fridge to serve within the next 24 hours.

Blackberry Pie

Similar to the cobbler, makes 2 pies

Ingredients:

  • 8? cups fresh blackberries
  • 2 pie crusts + 2 for lattice topping
  • 1 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 heaping Tbsp flour
  • 1 tsp cinammon
  • Dash of nutmeg
  • butter

Steps:

  1. Wash & drain blackberries
  2. Mix dry ingredients, add blackberries to coat.
  3. Place in pie crusts and dot with butter, add lattic topping.
  4. Sprinkle some extra sugar on top.
  5. Bake 400 for 55-60 minutes, may have to cover with foil to prevent over-browning/burning.
