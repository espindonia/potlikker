Martin Luther King, Jr.

1929-1968

Image source: www.nobelprize.org

Martin Luther King Jr, also known as MLK Jr, was many things. He was a pastor, a Civil Rights activist, a Nobel Peace Prize winner, and the victim of an assassination.

He is most known for his "I Have a Dream" speech, "Letter from Birmingham Jail," and his non-violent protests (like the bus boycotts).

Lyndon B Johnson

1908-1973

Image source: www.britannica.com

Lyndon B. Johnson, LBJ for short, is a former United States Vice President and President. He was a moderate Democrat, a former Senator, and was promoted to Presidency following the assassination of President John F. Kennedy. He is perhaps most known for signing the Civil Rights Act into law.

Robert F. Kennedy

1925-1968

Image source: www.britannica.com

Robert F Kennedy, also knwon as RFK, was a former US Senator and President with the Democratic Party. He was also a supporter of the Civil Rights Movement, and even sent hundreds of federal marshals to protect MLK Jr. and his supporters, following threats from a hostile mob.

Rosa Parks

1913-2005

Image source: www.britannica.com

Rosa Parks was Civil Rights activist, most well-known for peaceful protest on a bus in Alabama. She'd refused to give up her seat for a white passenger, leading to her arrest. She was a member of the NAACP, and worked on many civil rights cases with them, like the Scottsboro boys case of 1935. She recieved many medals before her death, like the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and even had an award named after her (the Rosa Parks Freedom Award).

Georgia Gilmore

1920-1990

Image source: www.npr.org

Georgia Gilmore, a lesser-known Civil Rights activist and cook, was a participant in the Montgomery Bus Boycotts. She ran a secret kitchen that helped feed the other participants of the Civil Rights movement, encouraging black women to sell well-known Southern foods like pork chops and pound cakes. The money that these women made was used to help pay for an alternative to the busses during the boycott, helping the boycott continue for over a year.

Emmett Till

1941-1955

Image source: www.nytimes.com

Emmett Till, a young African American teenager, is well-known for his murder when he was 14. Following an interaction with a white woman inside of a store, a group of white men (who were never convicted) lynched him, showing that the horrors of the racism in the South extended to children, a fact some were in denial of. It shocked the nation, further displaying the need for the Civil Rights movement and racial equality.

Stephen Gaskin

1935-2014

Image source: www.en.wikipedia.org

Stephen Gaskin was a member of the hippie counter-culture movement. He founded The Farm (members were the Farmees), a commune based on themes of community, veganism, and the use of weed and psychedelics.
